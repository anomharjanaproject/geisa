Methodology

Mixed methods were employed to capture a rich, intersectional perspective:

  • Qualitative: Key Informant Interviews (KIIs), Focus Group Discussions (FGDs).
  • Quantitative: Staff surveys analyzed with R-Studio.
  • Thematic analysis conducted using NVivo software.
  • Accessibility and safety measures ensured throughout.

Data Collection & Analysis

Data collection combined qualitative and quantitative approaches, focusing on gender, age, disability, and intersectional identities.

  • Qualitative coding and thematic analysis with NVivo.
  • Quantitative statistical analysis using R-Studio.
  • Ensured gender-sensitive, safe, and accessible facilitation.
  • Weekly Learning Circles for reflection and validation.

Key Findings

  • Overall high agreement with gender equality principles (85%).
  • Moderate neutral responses (10%) indicating areas for awareness improvement.
  • Minor disagreement (5%) on some institutional policies.

Recommendations

  • Enhance gender-sensitive training across all departments.
  • Increase accessibility measures for diverse staff needs.
  • Embed intersectionality more deeply into policy frameworks.
  • Regular participatory monitoring and evaluation.

Conclusion

The GEISA highlights strong organizational commitment while identifying opportunities to deepen inclusion and equity practices. Continued participatory efforts will ensure transformative and sustainable gender-just development.
