Methodology

Mixed methods were employed to capture a rich, intersectional perspective:

  • Qualitative: Key Informant Interviews (KIIs), Focus Group Discussions (FGDs).
  • Quantitative: Staff surveys analyzed with R-Studio.
  • Thematic analysis conducted using NVivo software.
  • Accessibility and safety measures ensured throughout.

Data Collection & Analysis

Data collection combined qualitative and quantitative approaches, focusing on gender, age, disability, and intersectional identities.

  • Qualitative coding and thematic analysis with NVivo.
  • Quantitative statistical analysis using R-Studio.
  • Ensured gender-sensitive, safe, and accessible facilitation.
  • Weekly Learning Circles for reflection and validation.

Key Findings

The survey results show strong agreement with gender equality initiatives:

Agree 85%
Neutral 10%
Disagree 5%

This indicates positive staff support and an enabling environment for transformative change.

Recommendations

  • Strengthen ongoing feminist participatory action research.
  • Enhance accessibility and inclusion in all programs.
  • Integrate GEISA findings into organizational policies.
  • Regular capacity building for gender equality facilitators.
  • Continuous monitoring and feedback mechanisms.

Conclusion

The Consultant GEISA report lays a foundation for transformative, gender-just development in Plan International Timor Leste, with participatory research and evidence-based recommendations.
